About

I created sciteit as a result of a journal club I was required to attend and participate in as part of my PhD in astrophysics. The idea of this journal club was that we would all keep an eye on the publications that were posted each week and pick something that was both of high quality and general interest. In astrophysics all new articles are posted on a pre-print server, arXiv, so in theory this was an achievable task. Although I had the best of intentions I rapidly found that it was too time consuming to properly read all articles that were posted each day. My solution to this was to "sort by brand" and focus on articles from big journals such as Nature or Science, which should (in theory) only publish interesting, high quality research. However, in so doing I knew I was missing out on a lot of interesting work that I would never see.

Essentially this is a sorting problem. There is too much published for one person to do a decent job of reading and deciding what is and isn't interesting. Furthermore, there is no central point for people to comment on an article. Instead, commentry and criticism is limited to personal discussions and individuals blogs, putting those not already "in the know" at a disadvantage.

These are the problems that I created sciteit to overcome. Articles are sorted by users directly voting them as interesting or uninteresting and a central location is available for comments. I am just one man, and I still have a PhD to do, so please be patient if something doesn't work properly and I'm slow in fixing it.
